Limits of Medical Advertising in Belgium

The Golden Rule

Objective informational communication is permitted. Commercial solicitation and patient-hunting ("Ronselen") are absolutely prohibited — risk of license revocation or disciplinary proceedings.

March 2024 New Obligation

All dentists must display rates in waiting rooms AND on websites using standardised INAMI stickers. Pricing ambiguity = immediate patient distrust and potential regulatory breach.

The Conventionné Split

In 2024, 50.4% of active dentists are Non-conventionné (vs 30% in 2013), entitling them to charge Suppléments d'honoraires not reimbursed by mutual funds — a major patient communication challenge.

Historical Turning Point · CJEU 2017

The Vanderborght Case — C-339/15

A single Belgian dentist's marketing campaign forced an entire nation to rewrite its advertising laws.

1934–1958
Absolute ban on all dental advertising. Only a small clinic sign permitted.
~2014
Vanderborght launches billboard, website & newspaper ads in Flemish Belgium.
2015
VVT files criminal complaint. Case referred to CJEU as Case C-339/15.
4 May 2017
CJEU rules absolute ban violates EU Directive 2000/31/EC & Article 56 TFEU.
2017→Now
Belgium liberalises rules. Honest & objective "commercial communication" now permitted.
1
↓
What Vanderborght Did
Launched a three-sided billboard, an explanatory website, and local newspaper ads — triggering a criminal prosecution by the Flemish Dental Association (VVT).
2
↓
What the CJEU Decided
An absolute blanket ban exceeded what was "necessary and proportionate." Goals like public health protection remain legitimate — but a total ban is disproportionate under EU law.
3
The New Reality
Belgium must permit "commercial communication" provided it remains honest, objective, and non-misleading. The spirit of anti-solicitation law still applies.
